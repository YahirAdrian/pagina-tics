Online Education

Published: 13/03/2023 Authors: Aylin Canul, Osmar Arjona, Valerio Vivas, Juan May

Online learning

Online learning (e-learning) describes any form of pedagogy delivered using digital technology. Such methods incorporate visual graphics, text, animations, videos and audio. In addition, online pedagogy can also facilitate group learning, and the assistance of instructors within specific fields (Wan Aziaris, 2015).

What's online education?

Online learning education offers several advantages, including flexibility in terms of time and location, personalized learning experiences, access to a wide range of courses and programs, and the opportunity to develop digital literacy skills. It can be particularly beneficial for individuals who cannot attend traditional in-person classes due to various reasons, such as distance, work commitments, or health issues.

While online learning education has its benefits, it also presents challenges. Technical issues, such as internet connectivity problems or limited access to devices, can hinder the learning experience. Students may need to develop self-discipline and time management skills to stay motivated and complete their coursework independently. Additionally, the absence of face-to-face interactions may impact social engagement and collaborative learning.

How widely used were online classes before the pandemic?

It is important to note that online classes were not as widespread or used in Mexico compared to traditional face-to-face instruction. However, the pandemic forced educational institutions across the country to quickly adapt to online learning models to ensure continuity of education during lockdowns and social distancing measures. Before the pandemic, online classes in Mexico were not as widely used as in other countries. However, there were certain cases where online learning was implemented to some extent.

Mexico had some virtual universities that offered degree programs online. These institutions provided an alternative for students who preferred flexible learning options or were geographically limited.

Some universities in Mexico have incorporated blended learning approaches, combining face-to-face classes with online components. This model allowed students to access course materials, submit assignments, and interact with instructors through online platforms. Educational websites and platforms were available to supplement classroom learning. Teachers and students can access educational materials, tutorials, and practice exercises online to enhance learning.

Online education platforms

During the COVID-19 pandemic, students had to adapt to online education, which posed major new challenges for the academic field. The use of online educational platforms was necessary to continue with classes, in this case, teachers also had to adapt to the new measures in order to be able to continue with their classes. But now, which applications were used in the academic field? A study done in Panama in which 14, 800 university teachers participated showed that the most used applications were Moodle, E-Educativa, Google classroom, Canvas and Microsoft Teams. (Universidad Católica Santa María La Antigua, s. f.)
