Advantages and disadvantages

Published: 19/07/2023 Authors: Aylin Canul, Osmar Arjona, Valerio Vivas, Juan May

Advantages and disadvantages

Advantages

Advantages of online learning during the pandemic included the continuity of education, ensuring the health and safety of students and educators, flexibility in terms of time and location, access to a wide range of resources, and the development of technological skills. It should also be noted that universal learning is facilitated thanks to online learning, which allows us to obtain almost immediate knowledge of many things by the simple fact of being able to do a search about it and find the answers we need, talking about universal topics we can also mention the fact of using talents from other countries without having to make long trips, with this we realize that a teacher who specializes in a certain subject can teach from the other side of the world without inconvenience and spread his knowledge to the whole world, this also adds one more point that would be saving resources, not spending money on travel, not buying physical material if you can have it in digital and better management of personal time.

Disadvantages

However, there were also disadvantages, such as limited interaction and engagement, technological challenges, pedagogical limitations for certain subjects, increased responsibility and self-motivation requirements, and concerns regarding assessment and cheating. Things like no internet connection or no technology means no learning, as well as the fact that any technical glitches affect the quality of the live class and generate more questions than answers. These advantages and disadvantages varied based on individual circumstances and the quality of online learning platforms and support provided.

Flexibility

Online learning provides the flexibility to study at one's own pace and convenience. Students can access course materials and lectures anytime, anywhere, allowing for personalized learning schedules. Pedagogical flexibility attempts to broaden and enrich the way of learning. In the light of new learning theories and Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs), it is inserted in a social context, under new learning models and new ways of creating, designing and building (Melo, 2019; Ruiz Díaz et al..., 2019). The students do not receive or listen, but rather create and do. The action is the main focus. Students ask, build, organize, present their arguments and solutions (Ruiz Díaz et al., 2019). Educational flexibility is the key action when teaching and learning in times of pandemic. Autonomous and motivated students are those who see in classroom activities (cases, laboratories, simulations, forums, etc.) opportunities to learn, understand and apply to the real and business world (Ruiz Gimenez & Martínez Jiménez, 2019).

Technical challenges

Online learning heavily relies on technology, and technical issues such as internet connectivity problems, software glitches, or hardware limitations can disrupt the learning process and cause frustration for students.

Firstly, ensuring reliable internet connectivity has been a significant issue. Many students, especially those in remote or underserved areas, have struggled with limited access to stable internet connections, leading to disruptions in class sessions and difficulties in accessing online resources.

Secondly, technical proficiency and digital literacy have posed challenges for both educators and students. Many teachers had to quickly adapt to unfamiliar online platforms and tools, requiring them to learn new technical skills to effectively deliver lessons and engage students virtually. Similarly, some students faced difficulties in navigating online learning environments and using the necessary software and applications, hindering their ability to actively participate in classes.

Addressing these technical challenges has required educators and institutions to provide support and resources to ensure equitable access to online education, including offering technical assistance, providing devices and internet connectivity to students in need, and implementing training programs to enhance digital skills for both teachers and students.
